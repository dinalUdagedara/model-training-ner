## 2. What each section must contain (from rubric text)

### Front matter & structure (0.1 band)

- **Cover page / project title:** numbered components present vs partial vs full.
- **Declaration, Abstract, Acknowledgements, TOC, List of figures, List of tables** — presence and quality.
- **Structured writing and style:** argument flow across chapters; connections between literature, methods, and discussion; higher bands = critical reflection and congruent research questions.

### Chapter 1 — Introduction (0.4 band)

Must include **1.1 Problem statement** and **1.2 Aims and objectives**.  
Bands: from unclear aims → clear project type and aims → successful FYP → connection to practice → difficult technical/research tasks → **contribution to knowledge/practice**.

### Chapter 2 — Background (0.4 band)

Subsections expected: **2.1 Literature survey**, **2.2 Review of project/applications**, **2.3 Review of tools and techniques**.  
Bands: weak background → outline → link to practice → similar applications → **critical** presentation of similar work → **reflective** critical comparison.

### Chapter 3 — Legal, social and ethical issues (0.4 band)

Cover legal, ethical, social, professional, **security** issues for research, software, data.  
Higher bands require **ethical approval** if you collected participant data, and **reflexivity** in methods/conclusions.

### Chapter 4 — Methodology (0.4 band)

Grounded methodology and design; justify choice; link design to requirements; systematic/critical design; **contribution** at top band.

### Chapter 8 — Conclusions & reflections (0.4 band)

Conclusions and reflections on the application, **new knowledge and skills**, **further work**.  
Top bands: concise conclusions tied to **aims**, limitations, **reflective** evaluation of process.

### Chapter 5 — Design (0.5 band)

Study design + design of models/software/infrastructure. Diagrams/prototypes; alignment with requirements; **originality** at high bands.

### Chapter 6 — Tools and implementation (0.5 band)

Must include **6.1 Tools used and justification**, **6.2 Implementation** (main code for key functions; **novel** vs **adopted/adapted** code with **sources**).  
Bands stress: explanation quality, execution quality, **gain in technical skills**, **critical evaluation** if core scope not fully implemented, **originality/contribution** at top.

### Chapter 7 — Testing (0.5 band)

**7.1 Functional testing** (black box and/or white box), **7.2 User testing**.  
Bands: none/basic → functional → multiple techniques → critical discussion + further development → **contribution** at top.

### Chapter 9 — References

Cited in-text; Harvard (or one style **consistently**).

### Chapter 10 — Bibliography

General reading (not only cited works).

### Appendices

Examples: requirement-gathering data, user evaluation data, extended code.

## 4. Important: chapter numbering vs your thesis template

Your **Word thesis template** (`[Template FYP -2025_26] Final Thesis .docx`) uses a **different** order (e.g. Testing as **Chapter 8**, References near the end, etc.).

**Action:** Map your template chapters to **what the rubric names** (Introduction, Background, SLEP, Methodology, Design, Implementation, Testing, Conclusions, References, Bibliography). If unsure, **ask your supervisor** which numbering the department expects on the cover/TOC for marking.
